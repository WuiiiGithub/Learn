Fundamental Signal Math

Interact with theoretical equations, observe the dynamic calculations, and understand the physical variables.

1. The Anatomy of a Wave

The fundamental equation mapping circular motion (phase) to linear amplitude over time \(t\).

Theoretical Formula

$$ y(t) = A \cdot \left( \sin(2\pi f t + \phi) \right)^p + D $$

Dynamic Calculation

Variable Glossary

  • \(A\) (Amplitude): The peak physical magnitude. Controls how tall the wave is.
  • \(f\) (Frequency): How many cycles occur per second (Hz).
  • \(\phi\) (Phase): Horizontal shift — where in the cycle the wave starts at \(t=0\).
  • \(D\) (DC Offset): Vertical shift of the entire wave. Moves the center baseline up or down.
  • \(p\) (Exponent): Morphs the wave shape. \(p=1\) → pure sine. \(p=2\) → all-positive squared wave. Negative \(p\) → inverted/divided. Non-integer → smooth distortions.

2. The Master Moment Equation

Variance, Skewness, and Kurtosis are the same equation raised to different powers. Upload a CSV or use the data generator to test it.

Theoretical Formula (Central Moments)

$$ M_p = \frac{1}{N - k} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left( \frac{x_i - \mu}{\sigma_{\text{scale}}} \right)^p $$

Dynamic Calculation (Variance)

Variable Glossary

  • \(N\): Total number of data points.
  • \(k\): Bessel's correction. (\(k=1\) for samples, \(k=0\) for full populations).
  • \(x_i\): The value of the signal at index \(i\).
  • \(\mu\): The mean (average) of the entire signal.
  • \(\sigma_{\text{scale}}\): Scaling denominator. For Variance (\(p=2\)): set to 1 (no scaling). For Skewness/Kurtosis (\(p>2\)): set to \(\sigma\) (std dev) so the result is scale-invariant and unit-free.
  • \(p\) (Power): \(p=2\) → Spread/Variance. \(p=3\) → Asymmetry/Skewness. \(p=4\) → Tail heaviness/Kurtosis.
